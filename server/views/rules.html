4. Licence conditions

These conditions apply to fishing licences:

  1. You can only fish with a licence if you’re the person named on it
  2. You must carry the necessary proof of your licence when you go fishing, and show it to our bailiffs if they ask
  3. The licence gives you the right to use a fishing rod and line – not the right to fish. You must have the prior permission from any owners of fishing rights before you fish
  4. You must read and comply with the freshwater rod fishing rules
  5. You can only use the licence while it is valid

Byelaws

You must follow national and local rules (byelaws) when freshwater fishing with a rod and line in England and Wales.

Read the local byelaws for your area to find out the:

  • areas in your region where you’re not allowed to fish
  • closed seasons (when you can’t fish) which apply to particular types of water and fish within your region
  • sort of tackle you can use for certain fish in your region
  • size of fish you can keep
Warning Before you go fishing, read all national and local byelaws.

Fishing in Scotland and Northern Ireland

In Scotland, you need a fishing licence for the Border Esk and its tributaries. You don’t need a fishing licence elsewhere in Scotland, but you do need to get permission. Find out more about fishing in Scotland.

You must get either a rod licence or permit to fish in Northern Ireland.

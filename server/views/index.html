1. Overview

You need a rod fishing licence to fish for freshwater fish, including salmon, trout, smelt and eel. This licence allows you to fish with a rod and line in:

  • England (except the River Tweed)
  • Wales
  • Border Esk region of Scotland

Depending on where you want to go fishing, you may also need to get local landowners’ permission.

Children under 12 don’t need a licence. However, if you take an under-12 child fishing and you help to hold the rod, you must buy a licence for yourself.

Warning You can be fined up to £2,500 for fishing or intending to fish without a rod licence.

Types of licence

There are 2 types of licence:

  • trout and coarse fish licences that let you fish for coarse fish, non-migratory trout, char, eel and lamprey
  • salmon and sea trout licences that let you fish for salmon and sea trout as well as all those fish covered by trout and coarse fish licences

Number of rods you can use with your licences

There are two trout and coarse licences, the first allow you to fish with up to 2 rods. The second allows you to fish with 3 rods.

Salmon and sea trout licences allow you to fish with 1 rod, or up to 3 rods for trout and coarse fish.

If you're fishing for coarse fish and trout, 'spod' or 'marker' rods don't count towards these totals, as long as they don't have hooks attached.
